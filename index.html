We save lives

It is estimated that 26 million people each year fall into extreme poverty in the world due to the impact of natural disasters. That is why we present Monitum Aqua, an early warning system for floods that will help people with limited resources to prevent disasters of great magnitude.

Glaciers and volcanoes can cause flooding, but there are none of those near the Amazon. The reason for this flood was the 1953 floods which caused the flow of 370,000 cubic meters of water per second. To get an idea of ​​the immensity of the river, it can be said that it is responsible for 20% of the planet's fresh water discharge into the oceans, so the destruction caused by its overflow is enormous due to the large amount of infrastructure and dams that are built around the river.

Hurricane Agnes touched the soil of Florida, United States, in June 1972. This hurricane took the lives of 129 people and left more than 11 billion dollars in material losses, a multimillion-dollar figure with which it surpassed Betsy (1965) until then considered the most expensive hurricane in the history of catastrophes in the Atlantic. Agnes' greatest damage was not caused by winds but by rains, which caused massive flooding in Pennsylvania, Virginia and New York.

The Armero tragedy, in Colombia, was the result of an avalanche of water and mud caused by the melting of the Nevado del Ruiz volcano after an unexpected eruption. The natural disaster caused the total destruction of the city and left a balance of 25,000 dead, among which was the girl Omayra Sánchez, who after dying 60 hours could not be rescued and died under the rubble of her house.

With winds of up to 250 kilometers per hour accompanied by extreme rainfall, Mitch was one of the deadliest hurricanes of the last 200 years (second only to the Great Hurricane of 1780). At least 20,000 people died, mostly due to the floods in Honduras and Nicaragua, but which also affected El Salvador, Guatemala and the Yucatan Peninsula.

The intense rains registered in December 1999 in Venezuela caused the so-called “Vargas Disaster”, the worst natural disaster suffered by that country during the 20th century. A series of landslides, landslides and floods in the Ávila mountain range left a balance of 30,000 dead and more than 140,000 affected. The event appears in the Guinness Book of Records as the greatest tragedy caused by a mudslide.

Intense rains registered between April and May 2003 affected the city of Santa Fe and surrounding towns, located 480 kilometers north of Buenos Aires. It was one of the most important catastrophes in the country due to its magnitude, since the overflowing of the Salado River left a third of the population of the affected province, dozens dead, and more than 120,000 evacuated. The water took several weeks to drain and left a bleak picture. In the photo, an aerial view of the Colón de Santa Fe football stadium covered by water.

After an earthquake measuring 9.2 on the Richter scale with its epicenter in the west of Indonesia, a wave of great height occurred that climbed to more than 30 meters in some areas and washed the coasts of Thailand, Indonesia, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, India and even distant countries like Somalia. The tsunami devastated the coastal areas of the Indian Ocean, causing damage of more than 10 billion dollars, in addition to 300,000 deaths and 1.8 million evacuees.

The state of Tabasco, to the southeast of the Mexican capital, is crossed by the two largest rivers in the country (the Usumacinta River and the Grijalva River) which, together with other neighboring streams, surpassed their historical maximums due to heavy rains in October 2007 and caused one of the worst floods in the history of Mexico. The dewatering of the Peñitas and Malpaso dams located in the state of Chiapas, neighboring Tabasco, contributed to the flooding of towns and agricultural areas that left more than 1 million people affected and 700 million dollars in material damage.

In 2010, Pakistan suffered one of the worst floods in its history and soon after, without having recovered yet, the monsoon rains hit the south of the country again, especially the Sindh and Balochistan regions. Entire villages were razed and floods affected large areas of crops, leaving more than 2,000 dead, 7 million people homeless and almost 20 million people affected. It was a devastating blow to the Pakistani economy due to the damage it caused to agricultural production and even threatened to unleash a food crisis.

This aerial photo shows a community located near the river in Sand Spring, Oklahoma, being submerged. The historic floods were due to a combination of heavy rainfall, dam failures and high river levels, according to ONAA. Thousands of houses, businesses and crops were damaged.
